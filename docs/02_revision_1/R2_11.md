Risposta al reviewer: 

To address the reviewer’s request for sensitivity analyses, we refitted the focal F0 and NNE moderation models while varying only the prior standard deviation on the γ moderation coefficients. Specifically, we compared prior SDs of 1.5, 3, and 6, corresponding to a tighter, default, and wider regularizing prior. The substantive conclusions were stable across these specifications. For Negative Affectivity × stress predicting F0, posterior medians ranged from 1.76 to 4.39, with PD ≥ .93. For Antagonism × recovery predicting F0, posterior medians ranged from 1.62 to 4.48, with PD ≥ .92. For Psychoticism × recovery predicting NNE, posterior medians ranged from 0.80 to 0.90, with PD ≥ .95. As expected, the tighter prior induced stronger shrinkage toward zero, whereas the wider prior yielded somewhat wider intervals. Importantly, the sign and posterior probability of direction of the three focal effects were unchanged across all refits. All models converged well, with max R-hat = 1.001, minimum bulk ESS = 11,390, and no divergent transitions. At the default prior SD = 3, the refitted models reproduced the estimates reported in the manuscript. We therefore interpret these effects as directionally robust to reasonable changes in the moderation prior, while retaining the cautious inferential language used throughout the manuscript because several credible intervals remained compatible with small or null effects.
